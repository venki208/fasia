ORIGIN OF FASIA

The journey of Indians into America was not an easy one,culturally as well as professionally. They've had to go through challenging times for more than 75 years to attain the current status. Like any other ethnic group, Indians also faced challenging times to get their first set of recognition as a community.

The first major influx of the Indians from Asia into the United States was between 1904 and 1911, when more than 6,000 Indians went to work as farmhands. Although, historical documents reveal Indians visiting for trade in 1635 & 1790 AD

Indians from Asia in the U.S. were first classified in court decisions of 1910 and 1913 as Caucasians, and therefore could become citizens as well as intermarry with U.S.- born whites. However, the decisions were reversed by the Supreme Court in 1923, when Indians from Asia were legally classified as non-white and therefore ineligible for citizenship. That court decision prevented Indian immigrants from naturalizing. New immigrants from India had already been prohibited by a 1917 law.

The restrictions were lifted after the passage of comprehensive immigration legislation in 1965. This one change in law opened doors for the large influx of highly educated professionals from India to the U.S. for skilled employment. Read more .. Read Less

In 2017, an estimated 4.1 million Indian Americans are living in the U.S., according to the Census Bureau’s American Community Survey. Indians are the third-largest group among Asian Americans and represent about 19% of the U.S. adult Asian population.

Studies conducted by the pew social studies revealed that Indian Americans are a highly culturally driven society than any other ethnic group in the US and are also the ones with a high household median income group.

Indian Americans stand out from most of the other U.S. Asian groups in the personal importance they place on parenting; 78% of Indian Americans saybeing a good parent is one of the most important things to them personally.

Nearly nine-in-ten (87%) adult Indian Americans in the United States are foreign born, compared with about 74% of adult Asian Americans and 16% of the adult U.S. population overall. More than half of Indian-American adults are U.S. citizens (56%), lower than the share among overall adult Asian population (70%) as well as the national share (91%).

Educational attainment, among Indian Americans aged 25 and older,seven-in-ten (70%) have obtained at least a bachelor’s degree; this is higher than the Asian- American share (49%) and much higher than the national share (28%).

 

Indo Americans have created a huge impact in the last 25 years in Technology , Healthcare, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship by touching day to day lives . In the last 2 decades Associations were formed to bring them together and make a collective contribution to the society.

AAPI formed in 1982 to bring the Physician Community together. As per the AAPI with more than 80,000 practicing Indian origin physician and additionally 40,000 fellow resident medical students at any given point of time are touching 1 in 7 American population lives and giving their services in the health care with a small population of medical practitioner but touching more than 30% of the American population. Indian origin physicians stand as the most served physician community in the United States.

ITSERV formed in 2007 to bring the Technology Ecosystem together. More than 400 out of 500 fortune companies of the United States is supported by the Indian team either in India or in America from research,innovation,customer support,and day to day business management using the technologies developed and deployed by Indian Americans. One in 12 entrepreneurs of Silicon Valley in 2016/17 are from the Indian American origin.

AAHOA was founded in 1989 to bring all the Hotel Entrepreneurs together. As per the AAHOA, Indian Americans in the hotel and motel industry faced discrimination, both from the insurance industry and from competitors placing "American owned" signs from them. This led to the formation of AAHOA - Asian American Hotel Owners Association in 1994 as a single national association. AAHOA made the Indian American community in the last 23 years to own more than 60% of the hotel industry(lodging) and contribute for the employment to economy . A single Indian community from Gujarat, Patels contribute to more than 60% of the motel industry valued to be 128 billion USD assets under their management.
